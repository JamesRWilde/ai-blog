## TL;DR

StackAI is an enterprise-focused platform that lets IT teams build and deploy AI agents through a visual, no-code interface — with governance, audit logging, and flexible deployment (multi-tenant, VPC, on-premise) baked in. Founded by PhDs out of Berkeley, it targets the gap between "interesting demo" and "production-grade AI that won't get your CISO fired."

## The Problem

Most enterprise AI platforms in 2026 fall into one of two camps: powerful but complex (requiring dedicated ML engineers), or simple but uncontrolled (shadow AI that compliance teams discover after the damage is done). IT leaders need a middle ground — something that lets business teams build AI-powered workflows while maintaining the security posture their board expects.

The agentic AI wave has made this worse. Chatbots that just answer questions are easy to govern. Agents that read documents, trigger workflows, call APIs, and take actions across your infrastructure? That's a different risk profile entirely.

## What StackAI Actually Does

StackAI positions itself as an "AI transformation platform" — which, translated from marketing, means: a visual workflow builder where you chain together data sources, LLMs, and actions into governed AI agents.

### Core capabilities

- **Visual agent builder** — drag-and-drop interface for assembling AI workflows without writing code. Think: connect a document loader → an LLM → a decision step → an email output.
- **Pre-built templates** — out-of-the-box configurations for common enterprise use cases (customer support agents, document analysis, risk assessment).
- **Multi-model support** — swap between different LLMs depending on cost, latency, or accuracy requirements.
- **Data loaders** — connect to enterprise data sources for retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) workflows.

### The governance angle

This is where StackAI differentiates from the no-code AI builder crowd:

- **SOC 2, HIPAA, and GDPR compliance** — not just "we're working on it," but actual certifications.
- **SSO and access control** — role-based permissions for who can build, deploy, and run agents.
- **Audit logs** — full visibility into what agents did, when, and with what data.
- **Deployment flexibility** — multi-tenant cloud, Virtual Private Cloud, or fully on-premise. This matters for industries where data can't leave your infrastructure.

### Pricing

- **Free tier**: 500 runs/month, 2 projects, 1 seat, community support on Discord.
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing with unlimited projects, dedicated infrastructure, solution engineers, and all compliance features.

The free tier is functional enough for experimentation, but the real product is the enterprise tier where the governance features live.

## How It Compares

In the 2026 enterprise AI platform landscape, StackAI sits alongside heavyweights like Microsoft Azure AI, Google Vertex AI, and Databricks Mosaic AI — but with a fundamentally different approach. The big cloud providers offer raw infrastructure and model access; StackAI offers a complete abstraction layer on top.

Compared to direct competitors like Dify (open-source, broader feature set but less enterprise governance) or traditional workflow automation tools adding AI (Zapier, Make), StackAI's bet is that **governance-first design** wins in regulated industries.

A [recent comparison](https://www.stackai.com/insights/top-10-enterprise-ai-platforms-in-2026-(ranked-compared)) on their own blog ranks StackAI among the top 10 enterprise AI platforms for 2026, citing "fast, governed AI app and agent delivery with workflow-first design."

## The Honest Assessment

**What works:**
- The governance story is real, not vaporware — actual certifications, actual deployment flexibility
- No-code approach lowers the barrier for IT teams (not just ML engineers)
- The visual workflow model maps well to how enterprises actually think about processes

**What to watch:**
- Enterprise pricing is opaque (no public numbers) — expect the standard "contact sales" dance
- The platform's power depends heavily on which LLMs it supports and how well the RAG pipeline performs at scale
- Being a smaller player in a field with Microsoft, Google, and AWS means ecosystem integrations matter — and that's a moving target

**Confidence level: Medium.** The product is real and the compliance credentials check out, but enterprise AI platforms are a brutally competitive market. StackAI's survival depends on execution speed and whether enterprises trust a startup over the hyperscalers for mission-critical AI workflows.

## Bottom Line

If you're an IT team evaluating AI agent platforms and your checklist includes "must not get us sued," StackAI is worth a serious look. It's not trying to be everything to everyone — it's focused on the boring-but-critical problem of making AI agents governable in regulated environments.

Whether it survives the hyperscaler squeeze is an open question. But the problem it's solving isn't going away.
